Baqaee & Farhi (2019) Nonlinear IO

baqaee_farhi implements the production-network decomposition of Baqaee & Farhi (2019, Econometrica), which characterizes how microeconomic productivity shocks propagate to aggregate output beyond Hulten's theorem.

using MacroEconometricModels
io = load_example(:wiot)
bf = baqaee_farhi(io)
report(bf)
        Baqaee & Farhi (2019) IO decomposition

  Sector          Domar λ   Influence   Upstreamness

  Agriculture      0.4878      0.4331         1.5842
  Manufacturing    0.9756      0.9869         1.3861

Domar weights and Hulten's theorem

The Domar weight of a sector is its sales over GDP, $\lambda_i = p_i y_i / \text{GDP}$. Hulten's theorem states that, to first order, the elasticity of aggregate output to a sector's productivity equals its Domar weight:

\[\frac{d\log Y}{d\log A_i} = \lambda_i.\]

domar_weights(io)
2-element Vector{Float64}:
 0.4878048780487805
 0.975609756097561
bf.first_order ≈ domar_weights(io)     # Hulten holds exactly at first order
true

The second-order "beyond Hulten" term

The second-order term is the Hessian of log output in log productivities, capturing reallocation. It is parameterized by production-substitution elasticities theta and consumption-substitution elasticities sigma. Under the Cobb-Douglas default (theta = sigma = 1) the term vanishes and Hulten is exact; with gross substitutes (theta > 1) the diagonal rises.

baqaee_farhi(io).second_order                  # zero (Cobb-Douglas)
2×2 Matrix{Float64}:
 0.0  0.0
 0.0  0.0
baqaee_farhi(io; theta=2.0).second_order       # nonzero reallocation
2×2 Matrix{Float64}:
  0.24994   -0.199952
 -0.199952   0.159962

Network centralities

The result also reports the influence vector $\beta' L$, upstreamness (row sums of L), and downstreamness (column sums of L).
