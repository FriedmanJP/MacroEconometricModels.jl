Environmental Extensions

Satellite (extension) accounts attach stressor flows — emissions, employment, materials, water — to an IOData table and support consumption-based accounting via the Leontief inverse.

using MacroEconometricModels
io = load_example(:wiot)        # ships with "employment" and "CO2" accounts

Intensities and multipliers

For a stressor account with flows F (stressor × sector), the intensities are $S = F\hat{x}^{-1}$ and the consumption-based emission multipliers are $M = S L$.

intensities(io, "CO2")
1×2 Matrix{Float64}:
 0.1  0.15
emission_multipliers(io, "CO2")
1×2 Matrix{Float64}:
 0.165017  0.20132

Footprints

The footprint is the consumption-based account $e = M Y + F_Y$ (stressor × final-demand category), with a per-sector contribution by_sector.

fp = footprint(io, "CO2")
fp.total
1×1 Matrix{Float64}:
 400.0

Adding an extension

add_extension! attaches a new stressor account and precomputes its intensities.

add_extension!(io, "water", [12.0 28.0]; stressors=["H2O"], unit=["Ml"])
intensities(io, "water")
1×2 Matrix{Float64}:
 0.012  0.014

API

MacroEconometricModels.add_extension! — Function
add_extension!(io, name, F; stressors, unit, F_Y=nothing)

Attach a satellite account name with stressor flows F (n_stressor × n) to io, computing intensities S = F x̂⁻¹. F_Y (n_stressor × n_fd) gives direct stressor flows in final demand (defaults to zeros).

MacroEconometricModels.intensities — Function

Per-unit-output intensities S = F x̂⁻¹ of extension name.

source
MacroEconometricModels.emission_multipliers — Function

Consumption-based emission multipliers M = S L of extension name.

source
MacroEconometricModels.footprint — Function
footprint(io, name) -> FootprintResult

Consumption-based account of extension name: total = M·Y + F_Y (stressor × final-demand category) and by_sector = M ⊙ y' (stressor × sector), where M = S·L and y is total final demand.
